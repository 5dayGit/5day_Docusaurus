My Calendar

Overview​

My Calendar helps you view all your scheduled tasks in one place.

It shows:

  • Tasks assigned to you
  • Subtasks assigned to you
  • Google Calendar events (if you have synced your Google Calendar)

My Calendar always opens in Month View and displays the current month by default.

You can scroll through past and future months, and access the details of any task or event directly from the calendar view.

My Calendar is available from the Home section and is designed to give you a clear timeline of your work.

Who Can Access​

All users can access My Calendar.

Users will only see:

  • Tasks assigned to them
  • Subtasks assigned to them
  • Their own connected Google Calendar events (only if Google Calendar integration is enabled and sync is turned on).

Tasks assigned to other team members will not appear in My Calendar.

How to Access My Calendar​

  1. Click the Home icon from the navigation panel.
  2. Select My Calendar from the Home menu.

You will now be taken to the monthly calendar view.

What You Will See in My Calendar​

My Calendar displays three types of items:

1. Tasks​

Tasks assigned to you with valid dates will appear on the relevant day(s).

Displayed information includes:

  • Task icon
  • Task title
  • Time (always all-day format: 12:00 AM – 11:59 PM)
  • Date range (for multi-day tasks)

2. Subtasks​

Subtasks work exactly like tasks and follow the same display rules.

3. Google Calendar Events​

These events will appear only when:

  • You have connected your Google account
  • You have enabled sync
  • The selected calendar(s) are checked in the sync panel

Each Google event appears with:

  • Event icon
  • Event title
  • Event time (as provided by Google Calendar)

Navigation in My Calendar​

Move Between Months​

You can switch between months using:

  • < to view the previous month
  • > to view the next month

There is no limit — you can scroll infinitely into past and future months.

Jump to Today​

Click Today to automatically:

  • Jump back to the current month
  • Highlight today’s date with a circle
  • Position the month so today's date is visible

Select a Month Quickly​

Click on the month name (e.g., “November 2025”) to open a selector and jump to any month.

Calendar Sync Panel​

If Google Calendar integration is active, you will see:

Syncing: X Calendar(s)

When clicked, a panel opens showing all Google calendars linked to your Google account, such as:

  • Primary Calendar
  • Holidays

Each calendar has a checkbox.

  • Checked → Events from that calendar will appear
  • Unchecked → Events disappear instantly from My Calendar

For more information, refer to the Google Calendar Integration help guide.

How Tasks and Events Are Displayed​

1. Tasks/Subtasks With a Single Date​

If only a due date or only a start date is present, the task appears on that day.

2. Multi-Day Tasks​

If a task spans multiple days (e.g., 02 Nov → 06 Nov), it will appear as:

  • A continuous bar across all days in the date range
  • A single unified bar that stretches across the calendar row

3. All-Day Time Format​

Tasks and subtasks always show: 12:00 AM – 11:59 PM

Google Calendar events display their actual time.

4. Items Without Dates​

If a task or subtask has no start date and no due date, it will not appear in My Calendar.

Hover Interactions​

When you hover over a task or subtask, you will see:

  • Task icon
  • Task title
  • Date(s)
  • Time (all-day)
  • An icon to open the task details

When you hover over a Google Calendar event, you will see:

  • Event icon
  • Event title
  • Date and time
  • An icon to open the event in Google Calendar

Opening Item Details​

Tasks/Subtasks​

Clicking a task or subtask opens the Task Details view inside 5day.io.

Google Events​

Clicking a Google event opens the Google Calendar event page in a new browser tab.

Adding New Items​

In the top-left corner of the calendar, you will find the Add Task button.

From here you can:

  • Create a Task
  • Create a Reminder

This uses the standard Add Task and Add Reminder flows.

Notes and Behaviour Rules​

  • Calendar is always in Month View.
  • Week numbers are not displayed.
  • My Calendar shows only your own tasks and subtasks.
